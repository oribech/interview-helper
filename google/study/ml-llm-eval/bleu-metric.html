BLEU Metric

Goal: Exact $n$-gram precision + Brevity Penalty.

1. N-Gram Precision ($P_n$)

$$P_n = \frac{\sum_{\text{n-gram} \in \text{Pred}} \text{Count}_{\text{clip}}(\text{n-gram})}{\sum_{\text{n-gram} \in \text{Pred}} \text{Count}(\text{n-gram})}$$

Clipped: Match count cannot exceed its max frequency in Reference

2. Brevity Penalty (BP)

$c = \text{length}(\text{Prediction})$    $r = \text{length}(\text{Reference})$

$$BP = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } c > r \\ e^{1 - r/c} & \text{if } c \le r \end{cases}$$

3. Final BLEU Score

$$\text{BLEU} = BP \cdot \left( \prod_{n=1}^{N} P_n \right)^{\!\frac{1}{N}}$$

Typically $N=4$, uniform weights $w_n = \frac{1}{N}$

TL;DR $$\text{BLEU} = BP \cdot \exp\!\left( \sum_{n=1}^{N} w_n \log P_n \right)$$

End-to-End Calculation Example (BLEU-2)

1. Sentences

Original (Reference): the cat is on the mat  ($r = 6$ words)
Prediction: the cat on the mat  ($c = 5$ words)

2. General Formula

$$ \text{BLEU} = BP \cdot \left( \prod_{n=1}^{N} P_n \right)^{\frac{1}{N}} $$

3. Step-by-Step Derivation

$$ P_1 = \frac{5}{5} = 1.0 $$

* $P_1$ (Unigram Precision): Prediction has 5 words. All 5 ("the"x2, "cat", "on", "mat") appear in Reference without exceeding their max counts. 5/5 matched.

$$ P_2 = \frac{3}{4} = 0.75 $$

* $P_2$ (Bigram Precision): Prediction has 4 word-pairs. 3 match Reference ("the cat", "on the", "the mat"). 1 misses ("cat on"). 3/4 matched.

$$ BP = e^{1 - \frac{6}{5}} = e^{-0.2} \approx 0.8187 $$

* BP (Brevity Penalty): Prediction ($c=5$) is shorter than Reference ($r=6$), so we penalize it using $e^{1 - r/c}$.

$$ \text{BLEU} = 0.8187 \cdot \left( 1.0 \times 0.75 \right)^{\frac{1}{2}} $$

* Substitution: Plug computed values into the general formula with $N=2$.

$$ \text{BLEU} = 0.8187 \cdot (0.75)^{0.5} $$

* Inner Product: Multiply the precisions inside the parenthesis ($1.0 \times 0.75 = 0.75$).

$$ \text{BLEU} = 0.8187 \cdot 0.8660 $$

* Apply Root: Take the square root (exponent $\frac{1}{2}$) of $0.75$.

$$ \text{BLEU} \approx \textbf{0.709} $$

* Final Result: Multiply the penalty by the grouped precision score to get the final scalar value.

What Does the Score Mean?

Reference: the cat is on the mat

Prediction BLEU-4 Why?
the cat is on the mat 1.0 Exact match
the cat sat on the mat ~0.33 1 word wrong → breaks 3 bigrams, most trigrams, all 4-grams
a cat is on a mat ~0.25 Right structure, 2 words differ ("a" vs "the"×2)
the dog sat under a table ~0.0 Only "the" matches, no n-gram overlap for $n \ge 2$
Rule of thumb:
• 0.6+ = Very good (better than many humans)
• 0.3–0.5 = Understandable, decent quality
• 0.1–0.3 = Getting the gist but poor phrasing
• <0.1 = Basically unrelated

Choosing $N$ (Number of N-grams)

$N$ Measures Use when
BLEU-1 Word accuracy (bag of words) Only care about vocabulary overlap
BLEU-2 + Word pairs (basic fluency) Short texts, simple tasks
BLEU-4 ⭐ + Phrase-level structure Standard default. Machine translation, summarization
BLEU-5+ + Longer structures Rarely used — too strict, most scores → 0
Key insight: Higher $N$ = stricter. $P_1$ checks "right words?", $P_4$ checks "right phrases?". BLEU-4 is the sweet spot — captures both vocabulary and fluency without being impossibly strict.
