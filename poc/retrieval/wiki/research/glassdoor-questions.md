## 📋 GENERAL FEEDBACK SUMMARY

### Interview Process
- **Rounds:** Typically 5–8 rounds total — recruiter screen → phone screen → technical rounds (stats, coding, ML) → product sense → behavioral/Googliness
- **PhD applicants** get 2 back-to-back technical rounds instead
- **Internal transfers** report a much smoother, more behavioral process
- **Timeline:** Average 35 days; some candidates reported 4–6 months

### What Candidates Said
- **Positive feedback:** Structured process, supportive interviewers, collaborative problem-solving, emphasis on real-world impact
- **Negative feedback:** Long waits between rounds with no updates, some interviewers impersonal or poorly communicating, luck-dependent in what topics appear
- **Tip repeated multiple times:** Know which team you're interviewing for within Data Science — the focus shifts significantly depending on team (analytics, ML, product)

### Most Common Interview Topics (by frequency across all 38 pages)
1. **A/B testing & experimental design** — appears on nearly every page
2. **Statistics fundamentals** — p-values, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing
3. **Tell me about yourself / past projects** — universal opener
4. **Coding** — Python or R, typically data manipulation + one algorithm question
5. **ML concepts** — feature selection, regression, classification metrics
6. **Product metrics** — how to measure success of a Google product
7. **SQL** — joins, GROUP BY, window functions
8. **Probability** — coin flips, conditional probability, distributions

### Key Preparation Advice (from candidates)
- Nail A/B testing cold — it appears in virtually every round
- Be ready to code: data manipulation (pandas/SQL), one stats simulation, one algorithm
- Know MLE, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing rigorously
- Prepare strong STAR-format behavioral stories
- Google sends a preparation guide through the recruiter — follow it carefully
- Know which Google product team you're targeting; product sense varies widely by team
